# Misspecification battery results

Generated by `studies/misspecification.py` (raw per-rep rows: `studies/results/misspecification.csv`). Design: K=20 states with true weights evenly spaced in logit space over [0.005, 0.95], 1500 PC respondents (15 pairs each, 22,500 rows), 2500 PHE respondents (3 questions each, 7,500 rows), PHE questions restricted to the mid-range anchor subset (true dw in [0.05, 0.70], 13 states) exactly as in `tests/test_recovery.py`. All 14 cells share one base configuration (PC slope=-1.0, intercept=0.3; PHE sigma=0.8) built from a single shared kwargs dict; each misspecified cell overrides exactly one knob. 15 seeded reps per cell (pc_seed = cell_idx*10000 + 2*rep, phe_seed = pc_seed+1, recorded in the CSV); `estimate_dws` failures (ValueError) are counted, never retried. Total runtime 226.8s. Metrics are over all K=20 states against truth; entries are mean ± sd over the 15 reps.

**PC scale heterogeneity (scale_sd).** Ranking survives: Spearman 1.0000 at both 0.3 and 0.6. Levels survive: max abs err 0.018 and 0.024 against a 0.019 baseline. The mechanism is visible in the anchor slope, which attenuates from -1.009 to -0.901 and -0.682 — respondent-specific scales flatten the pooled probit betas roughly uniformly, and stage C's anchor map estimates its own slope from the data, so pure attenuation is rescaled away (R^2 stays 0.997).

**PHE random effects (re_sd).** Ranking survives (Spearman 1.0000 at both severities) and levels survive: max abs err 0.019 and 0.021, anchor slope -1.020 and -0.992, R^2 0.997. The ignored within-respondent correlation just inflates the interval regression's fitted sigma, which is a nuisance parameter; state-level logit weights stay unbiased. The cost is efficiency (understated standard errors), not point estimates.

**PC lapses (lapse_rate).** Ranking survives (Spearman 1.0000 at 5% and 15%). Levels survive despite the strongest attenuation in the whole battery: coin-flip responses drag the anchor slope from -1.009 to -0.518 (5%) and -0.345 (15%), yet because the attenuation is uniform across states the anchor map absorbs it (R^2 0.995-0.996), leaving max abs err 0.025 and 0.031 and mean abs err 0.008. The stage-C rescaling is doing real work here — stage-A betas alone would be badly compressed.

**PHE lapses (lapse_rate).** Ranking survives (Spearman 1.0000) and levels survive: max abs err 0.021 and 0.023, mean abs err 0.005 and 0.007, anchor slope essentially unmoved (-0.995, -0.999), R^2 dipping only to 0.994 at 15%. Coin-flip PHE responses add symmetric noise to the stage-B logit weights over the mid-range anchor subset without shifting them systematically.

**PC position bias.** The most harmless knob tested: Spearman 1.0000, max abs err 0.018 and 0.019 (at or below the 0.019 baseline), anchor slope -0.997 and -0.959, R^2 0.997 at both 0.1 and 0.3. Because pair order is randomized uniformly, a constant first-position advantage acts like symmetric noise rather than a state-level distortion.

**PC curvature.** This is the violation that bites. Ranking still survives — Spearman 1.0000 at 0.05 and 0.9998 at 0.15 (an occasional adjacent-pair swap) — but levels do not: max abs err rises to 0.049 at curvature 0.05 and 0.161 at 0.15 (8x the 0.019 baseline), with mean abs err 0.043 and anchor slope pushed to -1.292. Curvature bends the affine theta-logit(dw) link that stage C assumes, and a straight-line anchor map cannot absorb a bend. Worse, the diagnostic stays quiet: anchor R^2 only falls to 0.985, far above the pipeline's min_anchor_r2=0.9 warning threshold, so a 0.16 level error would pass through with no warning at all.

**PC error distribution (logistic).** Ranking survives (Spearman 1.0000) and levels survive: max abs err 0.023, mean 0.006, anchor slope -0.929, R^2 0.997. A slope-matched logistic link is close enough to the probit that the misfit is second-order at these effect sizes; the small residual attenuation is again absorbed by the anchor map.

**Most damaging violation for the real survey: curvature in the PC utility link.** Every other knob tested — scale heterogeneity, random effects, lapses on either module, position bias, a logistic error law — leaves max abs err within 0.012 of the 0.019 baseline because it produces uniform attenuation or symmetric noise that the data-estimated anchor map absorbs. Curvature is different in kind: it violates the affine A-to-B link itself, produces level errors up to 0.161 while Spearman stays at 0.9998 and anchor R^2 stays at 0.985, and therefore evades the pipeline's R^2 warning. For the real survey this means (1) rankings from the PC module are trustworthy under every violation tested, (2) levels depend on linearity that R^2 alone will not certify, so the design should spread PHE anchor states across the full severity range and the analysis should test the anchor scatter for residual curvature (e.g. a quadratic term in the anchor regression) rather than relying on the min_anchor_r2 gate.
