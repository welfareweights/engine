# Changelog

## 0.1.1 (2026-07-17)

Audit-response release: every blocks-nightly and fix-before-scale finding from the 2026-07-16 referee-grade audit, fixed with regression tests that record the pre-fix behavior verbatim.

- Fixed: `bootstrap_dws` published standard errors roughly 30% too small — and correspondingly too-narrow intervals — whenever the input tables carried duplicate index labels, e.g. batches combined with `pd.concat` without `ignore_index=True`; point estimates were unchanged and no warning fired, so the corruption was silent. Resampling now selects rows by position, immune to index labels; output on unique-index input is unchanged (bit-identical at a fixed seed).
- Added: per-state support floor in `bootstrap_dws`. A state informed by fewer than `min_state_respondents` (default 10) paired-comparison respondents, or estimable in fewer than half the bootstrap replicates (`min_state_reps`), previously published a spuriously tight interval with no warning (a 1-respondent state's interval could be tighter than every well-supported state's); it now publishes NaN se/lo/hi with a warning naming each state and the floor it failed, and keeps the point estimate. New `weights` columns: `n_resp_pc`, `n_resp_phe`, `supported`.
- Fixed (F2): the anchor-curvature warning mis-sized its impact metric by 5-11% (absolute instead of signed slope in the line-inversion error), so bends materially above the declared 0.025 tolerance could pass silently; `curvature_impact` now equals the exact line-inversion error (verified against brute-force inversion in the test suite) and previously-missed bends fire the warning.
- Added (F4, F1, F3, F7, F8): `estimate_dws` and `bootstrap_dws` (and `holdout_fit`, `fit_pc`, `fit_phe`) now validate inputs up front with errors naming the frame, column, and cause. Previously: fractional y was silently truncated to 0 — a flipped answer — shifting published weights with no warning (F1); NaN responses were misdiagnosed as unanimity ("one-sided") or crashed naming neither state nor cause (F7); NaN or string-typed values bypassed the exact guards built to catch bad designs and surfaced far from the cause, or not at all — including a fully silent NaN `mean_loglik` from `eval_phe` (F4); empty or all-one-sided inputs crashed with pandas-internals errors, one of them right after a reassuring "dropping one-sided states" warning, where the all-one-sided case now raises a designed data-regime verdict (F8); and `bootstrap_dws(level=95)` burned every replicate before dying without naming the parameter, while level=1.0/0.0 published degenerate min-max or zero-width intervals as if legitimate (F3).
- Added (F5): the weights table now carries an `extrapolation` column (distance of each state's mapped position outside the PHE anchor range, in anchor-span units; also on the `bootstrap_dws` table) and `estimate_dws` warns past a new `max_extrapolation` threshold (default 0.5 spans, justified in its docstring) — weights beyond the anchor range rely on the fitted line where the curvature gate cannot see harm, and could previously exceed the pipeline's own 0.025 damage threshold with zero warnings.
- Added (F6): a state present in the PHE responses but absent from the paired comparisons now triggers a warning naming it; previously it vanished from the published weights with no log line (only a silent diagnostics entry).
- Fixed (F9): small-n_boot runs with successful replicates no longer raise a false "design is too fragile" error (the old gate was unreachable below n_boot=20 even at 100% success); the fragility gate now fires on the failure fraction with a message reporting the true counts, and small n_boot is documented as a smoke-run mode (dw publishes, intervals blank with the accurate n_reps message).
- Fixed (P6): `bootstrap_dws` is importable from the package top level (`from welfareweights import bootstrap_dws`).
- Fixed (P9): the `bootstrap_dws` docstring no longer carries a stale exhaustive pass-through kwargs list; it defers to `estimate_dws`'s signature.
- Docs: METHODS §5/§7-map, VALIDATION §7, and the typeset methods guide updated for the exact curvature metric and the extrapolation diagnostic; the new test files are listed in both audit maps.

## 0.1.0 (2026-07-16)

First release. The three-stage GBD disability-weight estimator (Salomon et al. 2012 replication): probit on paired comparisons, interval regression on population-health-equivalence responses, logit-space anchoring with Gauss-Hermite back-transform. Identification hazards raise or warn instead of passing garbage (separation, non-convergence, one-sided anchors, degenerate or bent anchor maps). Respondent-cluster bootstrap for uncertainty (coverage-tested), multi-survey pooling with cross-survey tau, held-out fit diagnostics, survey instrument builder and LLM-robust answer parsing. Validation without microdata: known-truth recovery, metamorphic invariances, link-robustness, misspecification battery, power curves, and recovery at the published GBD 2010 design scale; see `docs/VALIDATION.md` and the methods guide (`docs/methods.pdf`).
